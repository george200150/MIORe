Abstract

We introduce MIORe and VAR-MIORe, two novel multi-task datasets that address critical limitations in current motion restoration benchmarks. Designed with high-frame-rate (1000 FPS) acquisition and professionalgrade optics, our datasets capture a broad spectrum of motion scenarios, which include complex ego-camera movements, dynamic multi-subject interactions, and depthdependent blur effects. By adaptively averaging frames based on computed optical flow metrics, MIORe generates consistent motion blur, and preserves sharp inputs for video frame interpolation and optical flow estimation. VAR-MIORe further extends by spanning a variable range of motion magnitudes, from minimal to extreme, establishing the first benchmark to offer explicit control over motion amplitude. We provide high-resolution, scalable ground truths that challenge existing algorithms under both controlled and adverse conditions, paving the way for next-generation research of various image and video restoration tasks.

Methodology

Continuous Motion Amplitude Spectrum Captured in Our Novel Datasets, MIORe and VAR-MIORe
Diverse Motion Patterns Captured within Different Scenarios in Our Proposed Datasets
Previous works have limited environment variations. Our datasets are collected in situations ranging from ones in which entities do not move, to the ones in which the subjects are extremely fast. We consider that by including such examples, the models could be better trained for the real-life. This in turn creates a necessity to find the common denominator for both static and highly dynamic targets.
Motion Magnitude Distribution and Sharp-to-Blurry Frame Ratios
The MIORe dataset is our linking connection to related works. The underlying idea is to build the MIORe dataset in such a way that it is comparable to previous works. To do that, we choose to regularize the motion magnitude by aggregating an invesely proportional exposure time to the optical flow magnitude.
